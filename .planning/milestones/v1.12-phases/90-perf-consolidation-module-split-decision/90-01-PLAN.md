PROHIBITED on the annotation body (each is a documented pitfall):
- `@Tag(...)` — varies per consumer (Surefire untagged vs Failsafe `@Tag("integration")`); putting it on the annotation mis-routes Surefire targets.
- `@Transactional` — only V4MigrationSmokeIT carries it; putting it on the annotation forces transactional rollback semantics into every consumer.
- `@DirtiesContext` — evicts the shared cache key.
- `@DynamicPropertySource` — registers a `contextCustomizer` participating in `MergedContextConfiguration.hashCode()`; this is the Phase 86 Plan-02 fragmentation lesson in reverse and would re-split the consolidated bucket.

Cache-bucket identity (Spring Framework 7.0.5 Javadoc):
- `MergedContextConfiguration.hashCode()` is computed from `locations`, `classes`, `contextInitializerClasses`, `activeProfiles`, `propertySourceDescriptors`, `contextCustomizers`, `parent`, `ContextLoader` FQN — and explicitly EXCLUDES the test class. Two classes wearing identical composed annotations collapse onto one cache bucket.
